Connecting the research community.

Machine Learning at Berkeley's research division seeks to provide a platform for students and faculty to collaborate outside of the context of the many AI/ML research laboratories at Berkeley. Our projects and papers are proposed by students and then backed over the course of each academic year into fully fledged research teams. We believe that landmark research in machine learning almost always comes from left field, and we cherish and encourage novelty in our division.

Children's Primitives for Bayesian Program Learning
Eliza Kosoy (PhD Candidate). Gopnik Lab
Train a Bayesian Program Learning model proposed by Lake et. al. (2015) on characters drawn by children, assessing model performance as character quality deteriorates and how children's character primitives differ from adults.

Adversarial Attacks Against Watermarking Techniques for Neural Networks
Xinyun Chen (PhD Candidate). BAIR. Adv. Dawn Song
Given existing watermarking techniques that protect pre-trained neural networks from unauthorized use and copyright infringement, can we propose adversarial attacks that break them while developing novel, more robust watermarking techniques?

StarCraft 2 AI
While deep reinforcement learning has made a lot of advances in beating video-games, from classic Atari games to the ancient game of Go, these advances have not translated to significantly harder settings. Being one of the most complex real time strategy games, can we train deep reinforcement learning algorithms to play StarCraft 2?

Research
Quantifying the Memorization Effect
Ruoxi Jia (Post-Doc). Adv. Costas Spanos & Dawn Song
The memorization effect describes the phenomenon where training data is leaked into model parameters, allowing users to recover underlying training data. Given possibly sensitive data used to train such models (i.e neural networks for healthcare applications), can we develop easily computable metrics that quantify the memorization effect, enabling more robust and consistent privacy benchmarking across models?
read more
Applications
AutoQuad UAV Autonomous Exploration
ML@B x UAVs @ Berkeley
Autonomous task completion is the holy grail for UAVs. The first step towards that goal is to master autonomous exploration or path following with obstacle avoidance. Using reinfocement learning techniques and simulated outdoor environments in Unity, can we train an Autoquad drone to handle autonomous Point A to B navigation in real time using a single camera input?
